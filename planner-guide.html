How this system works
Three principles hold everything together. If you forget everything else, remember these.
💾
localStorage is active. Everything saves automatically in this browser. Export weekly from the planner sidebar as a backup habit.
1 One meaningful action per day is enough

Not ten. Not a list. One thing that moves something forward. Everything else is bonus. Your brain runs on completion, not effort. A finished small thing gives more forward momentum than a half-finished big one.

2 Every task belongs to exactly one container

There are 7 containers: Creation, External, Admin, Legacy, Self, Home, Money. When a task arrives, your only job is: which container? You don't have to do it now — just file it correctly. The anxiety of a disorganized list comes from tasks that live nowhere.

3 Time blocks outrank feelings

You won't always feel like doing Creation at 7:30am. That doesn't matter. The block tells you what mode you're in. Start the focus timer, open that container, pick the top item. Structure replaces the decision.

💡
The planner's job: tell you which container you're in right now. Look at the timeline → find the current block → open that container → pick the top item → start the focus timer → go.
The Containers
Where every task lives

Container 1
Creation
Building Escheron forward

What goes here: Engineering standards, software design, business strategy, proposals, machine concepts, business plan.

What doesn't: Replying to emails (External). Paying bills (Admin). Anything reactive.

How to work it: Peak-med block 7:30–11:30am. Pick one item before you sit down — ideally the night before. Do not open email first.

TSS-PROD-MCH-SW-001EE standardsInvestor pitchMachine design
Container 2
External
Tasks that exist because people exist

What goes here: Meetings, replies, coordination, follow-ups. If it requires another person, it's External.

How to work it: Midday block 1–3pm. Process in batches. Reply, delegate, or convert to a Creation task.

Email repliesCustomer callsSupplier follow-ups
Container 3
Admin
Recurring, mechanical, necessary

What goes here: Bills, compliance, scheduling, ordering. Mark tasks as Recurring with a frequency (Daily/Weekly/Monthly etc.) — checking them off archives the completion and auto-advances to the next due date.

How to work it: 25-min blocks at 11:30am and 3:30pm. Execute, don't decide.

Pay billsFile receiptsOrder partsBP check
Container 4
Legacy
Old, sticky, emotionally loaded

What goes here: Tasks sitting undone for weeks. Decisions you've been avoiding.

How to work it: 25 min only. Each item gets one move: ✓ Close (done/irrelevant), → Decide (make the call now), ↗ Delegate (assign it), ⊞ Break Down (split it), ⏱ Defer (set a future date — hides until then).

Stalled projectsUnmade decisionsOld promises
Container 5
Self
Health, ADHD, your own maintenance

What goes here: Frida appointments, BP checks, ADHD tasks, exercise, sleep, personal development. Tasks have sub-areas (ADHD & Frida, Health & Medical, Personal Development, Exercise & Sleep), urgency (🔴🟡🟢), a deadline, and can be Recurring.

When: Morning transition 7–7:30am, or before a deadline.

Frida prep formsBP readingClaude trainingExercise
Container 6
Home
Family, house, life logistics

What goes here: GTA search, kids/school, house maintenance, domestic tasks. Tasks have custom sub-areas (you define them), can be Delegated (tap ✎, set "Delegate to" — shows as ↗ Name badge), and can be Recurring.

When: 3:30–4pm Admin + Home block.

Rule: Do not act on Home tasks during Creation time. File it, return to work.

Container 7
Money
Finances, taxes, anything with a dollar sign

What goes here: Tax filing, invoicing, banking, financial planning. Tasks show a due date and can be Recurring with a frequency — checking off archives the completion and creates a fresh copy at the next due date automatically.

Why separate: Money tasks get avoided hardest. Their own container makes them harder to ignore.

    The Daily Ritual
    What to do at each moment
    The planner shows the block. Here's what to do when you arrive at each one.
    6:00
    Wake · Family Morning
    Don't check work on your phone. Family time. Take Vyvanse at 6:30 with protein.
    7:00
    Transition + Self Check
    Meds activating. Open the planner. Check Self container — Frida tasks, BP log, med streak. Write today's One Action. Move your body 5–10 min. Sit down.
    7:30
    Creation Block · 4 hours
    Open Creation container. Start the focus timer (25m suggested). Do not open email first. The first task sets the tone for the day.
    11:30
    Admin Container · 25 min
    Set timer. Execute the recurring checklist. Stop when timer ends. Monday has an extra Admin kickoff at 11am.
    12:00
    Lunch · Step Away
    Eat protein. Leave the desk. Real break between deep work and afternoon.
    13:00
    External Container · 2 hours
    Open email. Return calls. Process to zero — reply, delegate, file, or convert to a Creation task.
    15:00
    Legacy Container · 25 min
    Pick one item. Make one move: Close, Decide, Delegate, Break Down, or Defer. One item is enough.
    15:30
    Admin + Home · 25 min
    Admin second pass if needed, then Home container — house tasks, GTA search, kids logistics. Same energy as Admin: low-decision execution mode.
    16:00
    Light Creation / Review · 90 min
    Lower-intensity work. Meds tapering. Good for review, documentation, planning.
    17:30
    Shutdown Ritual · 10 min
    1. Write one completion.
    2. Set tomorrow's One Action — now, not in the morning.
    3. Close work tabs.
    4. Say out loud: "Shutdown complete."

    Without a hard stop, ADHD brains stay in half-work mode all evening.
    18:00
    Family Time · Protected
    No laptop, no "just checking one thing."
    21:00
    Wind Down
    Dim screens. Write racing thoughts to park them. Target 9:30–10pm sleep.
    📅
    Day variations: Monday has an extra Admin kickoff at 11am. Wednesday afternoon is Business Dev. Friday has a 20-min Weekly Review at 11:30am. Weekends have optional Creation windows and a Legacy slot. Override any day with ✏ Edit Schedule in the planner.
    Task Management
    What you can do with tasks
    Everything available on any task in the planner.
    ✎ Edit a task

    Hover any task to reveal the ✎ icon. Click to open the edit popover — change the text and all extras for that container type (recurring settings, urgency, sub-area, due date, move, delegate, etc.).

    ↗ Move to another container

    The edit popover has a Move to container section at the bottom. Select a destination and Save — the task moves to the top of that container. Container-specific extras are stripped on move, except recurring settings which carry between Admin ↔ Money.

    ⠿ Drag to reorder

    Every task has a ⠿ drag handle on the left (visible on hover). Drag to reprioritize within the list. Order saves immediately.

    ↻ Recurring tasks

    Admin, Money, Self, and Home tasks can be marked Recurring via the ✎ edit popover. Set the frequency: Daily, Weekly, Biweekly, Monthly, Quarterly, or Annually.

    When you check off a recurring task, it archives the completion and creates a fresh copy at the next due date. The completion history is visible in the edit popover. The recurring badge shows the frequency inline (e.g. ↻ Monthly). Overdue dates flag red.

    Legacy moves

    Legacy items have five move buttons inline — no need to open edit:

    • ✓ Close — done or no longer matters
    • → Decide — commit to deciding this session
    • ↗ Delegate — assign to someone else
    • ⊞ Break Down — split into smaller tasks elsewhere
    • ⏱ Defer — set a future date; item hides until then
    Home delegation

    Home tasks have a Delegate to field in the ✎ edit popover. The name shows as a blue ↗ Name badge under the task. Delegated tasks stay visible until checked off so you can follow up.

    Schedule
    Editing your daily plan
    Every day can be customized without touching the base templates. Overrides are stored per-date.
    ✏ Edit Schedule toolbar

    Click ✏ Edit Schedule above the timeline. The toolbar has four actions:

    • ＋ Add Block — pick type, title, sub-label, start/end time (15-min snapping)
    • ⚡ Preset Day — replace the whole day: Deep Work, Travel, Admin, Off, Meeting Day, Reset Day
    • ⚙ Edit Templates — permanently edit the Mon–Sun base templates
    • ↺ Reset to default — clears the day's custom override (only shown when overridden)
    Drag and resize blocks

    In edit mode:

    • Click (without dragging) → opens block editor to change type/title/times
    • Drag a block → moves it to a new time
    • Bottom grip → drag to resize end time
    • Top grip → drag to resize start time
    • × button (top-right of block) → deletes the block

    Changes save on release. Click Done to exit edit mode.

    Per-day vs. template overrides

    Per-day overrides apply only to that specific date — a "Custom day" badge appears when active. Template editing permanently changes the Mon–Sun defaults. Days already overridden per-date are unaffected until reset. The timeline auto-expands if any block is outside the default 6am–10pm window.

    ⚡
    Travel days: Use ⚡ Preset → Travel for a quick full-day swap. Gives you transit work blocks in the morning and arrival time in the afternoon. Adjust from there with drag.
    Decision Rules
    When you're stuck
    The situations that will actually come up.
    Something interesting surfaces during Creation time.
    Write it in the relevant container. Return to the current task.
    An email arrives that feels urgent.
    Unless someone is in danger, it waits until your External block. "Urgent" emails are almost always someone else's priority.
    The Admin container is overflowing.
    Work 25 min, stop. Can any of this be automated, delegated, or eliminated? Mark repeating tasks as Recurring so they self-manage.
    A Legacy task is too overwhelming to touch.
    Use Break Down. Write the smallest possible first action → that goes into Creation or Admin. Mark the original "broken down."
    A Frida appointment is approaching or you've missed doses.
    Goes in Self immediately. Med continuity affects every other container. Treat it like a client deadline. Set it Recurring if it's regular.
    A home task needs someone else to do it.
    Add it to Home, tap ✎, set Delegate To. It stays visible with a ↗ badge until done — tracked without living in your head.
    Nothing got done today.
    That's a data point. At shutdown: one sentence about what blocked. Set one action for tomorrow. Don't audit the system — just restart.
    You don't know which container a task belongs in.
    Use the Sort a Task tool. Or: person needed → External. New thing → Creation. Existing maintenance → Admin. Weeks sitting → Legacy. Health → Self. Home/family → Home. Dollar sign → Money.
    ℹ️
    The Friday rule: Weekly review is 20 minutes max. One question: what's the single thing to change next week? Not ten. One.

    Using the focus timer well

    The timer works best as a session container — you commit to working on one thing for a defined stretch. 25m is the default (Pomodoro), but deep engineering often benefits from 50m once you're in flow. The three recent custom slots remember your personal preferences automatically after you use the ＋ input.

    The timer doesn't track which task you worked on — intentionally. It's a time scaffold, not a tracker. The red now-line on the timeline is your ambient day progress indicator.
